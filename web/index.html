How We Evaluate Evidence: The Evidence Hierarchy

Tier 1 · Very Low

In Silico

Computational predictions and gene expression databases. Hypothesis-generating, not proof.

Tier 2 · Low

In Vitro

Cell culture experiments. Tests individual cells, missing the complex scalp environment.

Tier 3 · Moderate

In Vivo Animal

Animal studies showing whole-organism effects. Relevant, but mouse hair is not human hair.

Tier 4 · Moderate-High

Ex Vivo

Human tissue/follicle organ culture. Real human tissue, but lacks blood supply and hormones.

Tier 5 · High

Human Open-Label

Human trials without blinding or placebo. Real human data, but bias-prone.

Tier 6 · Highest

Human RCT

Randomized, controlled, blinded trials. The gold standard for proving treatments work.

Our Methodology

Every claim on this page is evaluated using a consistent framework. We identify each major claim made about a treatment in marketing materials or published literature, then trace it back to the specific experimental model that generated the evidence. Claims are categorized by evidence level (from computational predictions to randomized controlled trials) and assessed for how much weight they deserve for hair loss decisions.

We use a 6-tier evidence hierarchy ranging from in silico (computational) at the lowest level to human randomized controlled trials at the highest. Each claim receives an evidence weight that reflects not just the type of study, but its quality, relevance to hair loss specifically, and any limitations in design or interpretation.
