# TDM-105795 for Hair Loss: What the Science Actually Shows

*A claim-by-claim breakdown of the evidence behind this first-in-class thyromimetic for androgenetic alopecia, with the experimental model behind each finding and what it means for you.*

---

TDM-105795 is a first-in-class topical small molecule thyromimetic being developed by Technoderma Medicines (Chengdu, China) for male androgenetic alopecia. Unlike finasteride and dutasteride (which block DHT) or minoxidil (which acts as a potassium channel opener and vasodilator), TDM-105795 works through thyroid hormone receptor signaling -- a completely novel mechanism of action for hair loss treatment. It has completed Phase 2a testing in the U.S. with positive results announced in February 2024.

This is a genuinely new approach. But "new mechanism" and "proven treatment" are different things. Here is every major claim about TDM-105795, matched to the actual evidence behind it.

---

## How to Read Scientific Evidence: A Quick Hierarchy

Before we get into the claims, you need to understand the evidence ladder. Not all studies are created equal:

| Evidence Level | What It Is | Weight for a Consumer | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **In silico** (computational) | Computer analysis of gene expression databases | Very Low | Predicts what *might* happen based on patterns. No biological system was actually tested. |
| **In vitro** (cell culture) | Cells grown in a dish, treated with a compound | Low | Shows biological activity in isolated cells, but cells in a plastic dish don't behave like cells in your scalp. |
| **In vivo animal** | Living mice, rats, minipigs, etc. | Moderate | Whole-organism data, which matters. But animal skin is not human skin. Mouse hair cycles are synchronized in ways human hair cycles are not. |
| **Ex vivo** (organ culture) | Real human tissue maintained alive outside the body | Moderate-High | Tissue architecture is intact, but no blood flow, hormones, or immune context. |
| **Human clinical trial (open-label)** | People use the product and results are measured, but no placebo group | High | Real human data. But without a placebo control, you can't separate treatment effect from natural variation. |
| **Human RCT (double-blind, placebo-controlled)** | Gold standard. Patients get treatment or placebo, and neither they nor doctors know which. | Highest | The only design that definitively proves a treatment works. |

Keep this ladder in mind as we go through each claim.

## Claim 1: "TDM-105795 is a thyromimetic that activates dormant hair follicle stem cells"

**The model:** Preclinical pharmacology characterization (specific assay details not published in peer-reviewed literature; data reported in company press releases and regulatory filings).

**The finding:** Technoderma describes TDM-105795 as a thyroid hormone receptor beta-1 (TRbeta1) selective agonist -- a synthetic molecule that mimics the action of thyroid hormones at the hair follicle. According to the company, TDM-105795 binds to hair follicle cell target receptor proteins, activates dormant hair follicle stem cells, and induces the transition from telogen (resting phase) to anagen (growth phase).

**How much weight to give it: Moderate for the biological rationale, but low for the specific compound claims because the primary data has not been published.** The *class-level* science supporting thyromimetics for hair growth is strong (see Claims 2 and 3 below). But Technoderma has not published the receptor binding data, selectivity profile, or detailed pharmacology of TDM-105795 in a peer-reviewed journal. What we have are press release descriptions. The mechanism is plausible and consistent with published thyroid hormone biology, but the specific compound-level evidence remains proprietary.

**Citation:** Technoderma Medicines press releases, February 2024 and April 2023 (PRNewswire); Synapse PatSnap drug profile.

## Claim 2: "Thyroid hormone signaling controls hair follicle stem cell function"

**The model:** In vivo -- Genetically modified mice lacking thyroid hormone receptors (TRalpha1 and TRbeta knockout mice). Researchers at the Spanish National Cancer Research Centre studied the hair follicle stem cells in the bulge region of these mice.

**The finding:** Mice lacking thyroid hormone receptors showed epigenetic alterations in their bulge stem cells and a functional defect in stem cell mobilization out of the niche. This was linked to aberrant activation of Smad signaling and reduced nuclear beta-catenin accumulation. Thyroid hormone signaling induces a widespread change in chromatin state that is permissive for transcription and stem cell mobilization -- essentially, thyroid hormones help "unlock" the hair follicle stem cells so they can be recruited into a new growth cycle.

**How much weight to give it: Moderate.** This is a well-designed genetic study in a whole-organism model with a clear mechanistic finding. It establishes that thyroid hormone signaling is *necessary* for normal hair follicle stem cell function. The limitation is that it's a mouse model, and mouse hair cycling differs from human hair cycling. But as mechanistic evidence supporting the thyromimetic approach, this is solid.

**Citation:** Contreras-Jurado et al., "Thyroid hormone signaling controls hair follicle stem cell function." Mol Biol Cell. 2015;26(7):1263-1272. PMC4454174.

## Claim 3: "Thyroid hormones directly promote hair growth in human hair follicles"

**The model:** Ex vivo -- Microdissected human scalp hair follicles maintained in organ culture. Researchers at the University of Lubeck treated these follicles with T3 and T4 (the two main thyroid hormones) and measured multiple hair biology parameters over 6 days.

**The finding:** T4 up-regulated proliferation of hair matrix keratinocytes. Both T3 and T4 down-regulated apoptosis (programmed cell death) in the hair follicle. T4 prolonged the duration of anagen, possibly through down-regulation of TGF-beta2, a key anagen-inhibitory growth factor. Human hair follicles also express deiodinase enzymes (D2 and D3), meaning they can locally convert T4 to the more active T3.

**How much weight to give it: Moderate-High.** This is the first direct evidence that human hair follicles are targets of thyroid hormones. The ex vivo human hair follicle model preserves tissue architecture and uses actual human tissue, making it considerably more relevant than cell culture. Multiple hair biology parameters responded to thyroid hormone treatment. The limitation is that organ culture lacks blood supply and systemic context, so you cannot extrapolate directly to what happens when a topical thyromimetic is applied to a living scalp. But as evidence that the *target* (human hair follicle thyroid hormone receptors) exists and responds, this is strong.

**Citation:** van Beek et al., "Thyroid hormones directly alter human hair follicle functions: anagen prolongation and stimulation of both hair matrix keratinocyte proliferation and hair pigmentation." J Clin Endocrinol Metab. 2008;93(11):4381-4388. PMID: 18728176.

## Claim 4: "A TRbeta-selective thyromimetic promoted hair growth in animal models"

**The model:** In vivo -- C3H mice (a standard model for hair cycle research) and stumptail macaque monkeys. Researchers at a pharmaceutical company tested a thyroid hormone receptor beta subtype-selective thyromimetic topically.

**The finding:** The TRbeta-selective agonist was efficacious in both mouse and monkey hair growth models after topical application. This established proof-of-concept for the thyromimetic mechanism across species, including a primate model that more closely resembles human hair biology.

**How much weight to give it: Moderate.** Mouse studies alone are notoriously poor predictors of human hair loss treatment efficacy (the C3H model has a synchronized hair cycle that does not resemble human pattern baldness). But the stumptail macaque is considered one of the best animal models for androgenetic alopecia -- these monkeys naturally develop pattern hair loss similar to humans. Positive results in both species strengthen the case considerably. The limitation is that this was not TDM-105795 specifically; it was a different TRbeta agonist. It supports the *mechanism*, not the specific compound.

**Citation:** Li J & Mitchell LH, "Thyroid receptor agonists for the treatment of androgenetic alopecia." Bioorg Med Chem Lett. 2009;19(24):6977-6981. PMID: 19900809.

## Claim 5: "TDM-105795 stimulated dose-dependent hair growth in mice"

**The model:** In vivo -- C3H mice treated topically with TDM-105795 at various doses.

**The finding:** TDM-105795 potently stimulated hair growth in a dose-dependent manner when applied topically to C3H mice. The compound was able to induce anagen in telogen-phase hair follicles.

**How much weight to give it: Moderate, but with significant caveats.** Dose-dependent efficacy in mice is a standard preclinical gate that TDM-105795 cleared. It shows the compound is biologically active when applied topically, which is not a given for every thyromimetic. However, the detailed data (doses used, quantitative hair growth measurements, comparison to positive controls like minoxidil) has not been published in a peer-reviewed journal. This data comes from company disclosures in press releases and regulatory filings. The C3H mouse model, while standard, has well-known limitations for predicting human AGA outcomes.

**Citation:** Technoderma Medicines press release, April 2023 (PRNewswire); ClinicalTrials.gov NCT04913519 background.

## Claim 6: "TDM-105795 showed favorable safety in animal toxicology studies"

**The model:** In vivo -- Rats and minipigs (standard species for dermal toxicology assessment).

**The finding:** Testing in rats and minipigs demonstrated favorable toxicology and toxicokinetic profiles. Preclinical assessment indicated that TDM-105795 has poor systemic absorption following topical application and a short elimination half-life -- features designed to keep the compound's activity local to the scalp and avoid unwanted systemic thyroid effects.

**How much weight to give it: Moderate.** This is exactly the kind of preclinical safety data you want to see for a thyromimetic being applied topically. Systemic thyroid hormone effects (tachycardia, weight loss, bone loss) would be a serious concern for any thyromimetic, so demonstrating minimal systemic absorption is critical. The fact that the compound was designed to have poor systemic absorption and short half-life suggests deliberate molecular engineering for topical safety. Again, detailed data is not published in peer-reviewed literature.

**Citation:** Technoderma Medicines press releases, April 2023 and February 2024 (PRNewswire).

## Claim 7: "Phase 1 clinical trials confirmed TDM-105795 is safe and well-tolerated in humans"

**The model:** Human Phase 1 clinical trials. Two studies were conducted:

1. **NCT04913519** (Phase 1a, SAD -- Single Ascending Dose): A randomized, double-blind, vehicle-controlled, parallel group, dose escalation study evaluating safety, tolerability, and pharmacokinetics of a single topical application in healthy male subjects (ages 18-55) with androgenetic alopecia. Conducted at Therapeutics Clinical Research center, San Diego. Completed 2022.

2. **NCT05244980** (Phase 1b, MAD -- Multiple Ascending Dose): A randomized, double-blind, vehicle-controlled study of 28 days of daily topical application. 32 subjects across two U.S. sites (J&S Studies in College Station, TX and TCR Medical in San Diego, CA). Completed March 2023.

**The finding:** Phase 1 testing showed TDM-105795 to have a favorable safety profile and to be well-tolerated. No results have been posted on ClinicalTrials.gov, and no peer-reviewed publication of Phase 1 data has appeared.

**How much weight to give it: High for safety (Phase 1 is specifically designed for this), but limited by lack of published detail.** The fact that the compound progressed from Phase 1a to Phase 1b to Phase 2a without safety holds is a positive signal -- the FDA would not have allowed this progression if there were material safety concerns. But without published data, we cannot evaluate the specific adverse event profile, the pharmacokinetic parameters, or the systemic exposure levels in detail.

**Citation:** ClinicalTrials.gov NCT04913519, NCT05244980; Technoderma Medicines press releases.

## Claim 8: "Phase 2a trial demonstrated hair growth in androgenetic alopecia"

**The model:** Human Phase 2a randomized, double-blind, vehicle-controlled trial (NCT05802173). This is the most important piece of evidence for TDM-105795.

**Trial details:**
- 71 male patients with mild to moderate androgenetic alopecia
- 13 U.S. clinical sites
- Randomized 1:1:1 to high dose (0.02%), low dose (0.0025%), or vehicle (placebo)
- Once-daily topical application to the scalp hair loss area
- 4 months (16 weeks) of treatment
- Primary endpoint: Non-vellus Target Area Hair Count (TAHC) change from baseline in a 1 cm2 area

**The finding:** Mean change from baseline in non-vellus TAHC:
- **High dose (0.02%):** +24.3 hairs/cm2
- **Low dose (0.0025%):** +20.3 hairs/cm2
- **Placebo:** +14.0 hairs/cm2

Both TDM-105795 concentrations were well tolerated with no material safety issues identified. Bioanalytical testing confirmed extremely low to no systemic exposure following once-daily topical application.

**How much weight to give it: Moderate-High, but with important context.**

This is a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial -- the gold standard study design. That matters. But several factors prevent this from being conclusive:

**The placebo response was high.** A +14.0 hair/cm2 change in the placebo group is substantial and somewhat unusual. This means the *net treatment effect* over placebo was +10.3 hairs/cm2 for the high dose and +6.3 hairs/cm2 for the low dose. These are modest improvements.

**The trial was small.** 71 patients split three ways means roughly 23-24 patients per arm. Small trials are susceptible to noise. Statistical significance details (p-values, confidence intervals) have not been disclosed in the press releases, which is a notable omission -- companies that achieve clear statistical significance typically report it.

**The trial was short.** Four months is a standard Phase 2a duration, but hair growth treatments typically need 6-12 months to show full effects. Minoxidil and finasteride RCTs usually run 12+ months. TDM-105795 may show greater separation from placebo with longer treatment.

**No peer-reviewed publication.** As of March 2026, the Phase 2a results have been reported only via press releases. No peer-reviewed manuscript with full methodology, statistical analysis, and detailed adverse event data has been published. This means the data cannot be independently scrutinized.

**For context:** In comparable 16-week studies, 5% topical minoxidil typically produces TAHC increases of +20 to +26 hairs/cm2 in total (with placebo responses of +3 to +10 hairs/cm2). TDM-105795's absolute numbers are in a similar range, but its unusually high placebo response narrows the net treatment effect.

**Citation:** Technoderma Medicines press release, February 5, 2024 (PRNewswire); ClinicalTrials.gov NCT05802173.

## Claim 9: "TRbeta agonists could be superior to existing treatments because they directly modulate the hair cycle"

**The model:** This is a theoretical claim supported by mechanistic evidence, not a head-to-head clinical comparison.

**The reasoning:** Unlike minoxidil (which primarily acts through vasodilation and potassium channel opening) and finasteride/dutasteride (which block DHT synthesis), thyroid hormone receptor agonists directly engage pathways that control the hair cycle: Wnt/beta-catenin signaling, Sonic hedgehog signaling, BMP inhibition, and stem cell mobilization. The Wnt/beta-catenin pathway in particular is the master switch for anagen induction, and TRbeta agonists can activate it directly.

**How much weight to give it: Low as a superiority claim, moderate as a rationale for a complementary mechanism.** The mechanistic argument is scientifically sound -- TRbeta agonists do engage different pathways than existing treatments, which is potentially valuable. But "different mechanism" does not mean "better." Minoxidil and finasteride/dutasteride have decades of RCT evidence, long-term safety data, and millions of patient-years of real-world experience. TDM-105795 has one small Phase 2a trial. The theoretical advantage means nothing until it is demonstrated in larger, longer clinical trials with head-to-head comparisons.

That said, a novel mechanism raises an interesting possibility: TDM-105795 could potentially be *combined* with existing treatments (minoxidil, finasteride) for additive or synergistic effects, since it acts through non-overlapping pathways. This has not been tested.

**Citation:** Li & Mitchell, 2009 (Bioorg Med Chem Lett); Contreras-Jurado et al., 2015 (Mol Biol Cell); van Beek et al., 2008 (J Clin Endocrinol Metab); Thyroid receptor beta review: Drug Discov Today. 2024;29(6). ScienceDirect.

## The Evidence That's Missing

After matching every claim to its model, here's what the evidence picture looks like:

**What exists:**
- Strong class-level mechanistic science supporting thyromimetics for hair growth (published peer-reviewed papers from multiple independent groups)
- Preclinical animal data showing dose-dependent efficacy (company-reported, not peer-reviewed)
- Phase 1 safety data in humans (completed, not published)
- One Phase 2a RCT showing a positive signal over placebo (company-reported, not peer-reviewed)

**What does not exist:**
- **Any peer-reviewed publication of TDM-105795-specific data.** As of March 2026, all compound-specific evidence comes from Technoderma Medicines press releases and ClinicalTrials.gov registry entries. No independent research group has studied TDM-105795.
- **Statistical analysis of the Phase 2a results.** P-values, confidence intervals, and effect sizes have not been disclosed.
- **Long-term efficacy data.** The longest human trial was 4 months. We do not know if efficacy increases, plateaus, or diminishes with continued use.
- **Head-to-head comparison with minoxidil or finasteride.** The claim that TDM-105795 offers advantages over existing treatments is theoretical.
- **Phase 2b or Phase 3 trial data.** The compound has not yet been tested in a larger, longer confirmatory trial.
- **Global photography or patient-reported outcomes.** The Phase 2a data consists only of TAHC measurements. No standardized photos or patient satisfaction data have been released.
- **Published receptor selectivity data.** The TRbeta1 selectivity claim has not been characterized in a published paper, so the degree of selectivity over TRalpha (which mediates cardiac effects) is unknown to outside scientists.

## The Bottom Line

TDM-105795 represents the most advanced clinical-stage thyromimetic being developed specifically for androgenetic alopecia. The biological rationale is strong: thyroid hormone signaling genuinely controls hair follicle stem cell function, and published research from independent groups confirms that thyroid hormones directly promote hair growth in human follicles. The novel mechanism (TRbeta1 agonism) is differentiated from all existing approved treatments, which is scientifically meaningful.

The Phase 2a data shows a real signal -- a randomized, placebo-controlled trial found more hair growth with TDM-105795 than with placebo. The safety profile appears clean so far, with minimal systemic exposure. These are necessary ingredients for a promising investigational compound.

But the evidence is still early-stage. One small, short, company-sponsored trial with no published peer-reviewed data, no disclosed statistical analysis, a high placebo response, and a modest net treatment effect does not make a proven therapy. The compound has not been tested in Phase 2b or Phase 3. No independent research group has studied it. No long-term safety or efficacy data exists. The development timeline shows a roughly two-year gap between Phase 2a completion and March 2026 with no public update on Phase 2b/3 plans, which may indicate funding, regulatory, or strategic delays.

**The strongest honest claim you can make about TDM-105795:** It is a scientifically well-founded investigational compound backed by a novel mechanism with genuine biological support and one small positive clinical trial. It is a treatment to watch closely as development continues -- but it is not available, not proven, and years away from potential regulatory approval. Patients should not delay or replace proven treatments (minoxidil, finasteride, dutasteride) based on TDM-105795's early results.
